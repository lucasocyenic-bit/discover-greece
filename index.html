Through the Ages

Ancient History

Walk through millennia of civilization, from the dawn of democracy to the height of classical art and philosophy.

3000 BC

The Minoan Civilization

Europe's first advanced civilization flourished on Crete, building magnificent palaces and developing a sophisticated maritime trade network across the Mediterranean.

508 BC

Birth of Democracy

Cleisthenes reforms Athens, establishing the world's first democracy. The Agora becomes the beating heart of political life, philosophy, and public discourse.

447 BC

The Parthenon

Pericles commissions the Parthenon, a masterpiece of Doric architecture that would become the enduring symbol of classical Greece and Western civilization.

336 BC

Alexander the Great

From Macedonia, Alexander conquers an empire stretching from Greece to India, spreading Hellenistic culture across three continents and reshaping the ancient world.

Living Heritage

Greek Culture

Immerse yourself in traditions that have endured for thousands of years, from ancient philosophy to modern Mediterranean life.

Philosophy

From Socrates to Aristotle, Greece birthed Western philosophy. Walk the same streets where great minds questioned existence and shaped human thought.

Mythology

The gods of Olympus, heroic legends, and epic tales form the foundation of Western storytelling. Every mountain and sea has its myth.

Cuisine

Mediterranean flavors perfected over millennia. Olive oil, feta, fresh seafood, and herbs create a culinary tradition recognized by UNESCO.

Music

From ancient lyres to modern bouzouki, Greek music carries the soul of the nation. Rebetika, traditional folk, and contemporary sounds echo through tavernas.

Hospitality

Philoxenia — the love of strangers — is sacred in Greece. Guests are treated like family, a tradition rooted in ancient belief that gods walk among mortals.

Architecture

From Doric temples to Cycladic whitewashed villages, Greek architecture blends function with breathtaking beauty across millennia of innovation.

Questions

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before embarking on your Greek adventure.

The ideal time to visit Greece is from late April to early October. May, June, and September offer the perfect balance of warm weather, fewer crowds, and lower prices. July and August are peak season with the hottest temperatures and most tourists. For island hopping, late spring and early fall provide the most pleasant sailing conditions.

Greece is part of the Schengen Area. Citizens of the EU, USA, Canada, Australia, and many other countries can enter visa-free for up to 90 days within a 180-day period. Always check the latest requirements with your local Greek embassy or consulate before traveling, as regulations may change.

Ferries are the primary mode of transportation between islands, with frequent connections during the summer months. High-speed ferries can cover long distances in just a few hours. For shorter hops, local boats and water taxis are available. Renting a car or scooter on larger islands like Crete and Rhodes gives you the freedom to explore at your own pace.

Pack light, breathable clothing for the warm Mediterranean climate. Essentials include comfortable walking shoes (especially for archaeological sites), a wide-brimmed hat, high-SPF sunscreen, and a reusable water bottle. For evenings, bring a light jacket or wrap. If visiting churches or monasteries, modest clothing covering shoulders and knees is required.

Greece offers options for every budget. While luxury resorts in Santorini and Mykonos can be pricey, mainland Greece and lesser-known islands are quite affordable. A mid-range budget of 80-150 EUR per day covers comfortable accommodation, delicious meals, and local transportation. Street food like gyros and souvlaki offer delicious meals for under 5 EUR.

Beyond the famous moussaka and souvlaki, try spanakopita (spinach pie), dolmades (stuffed grape leaves), fresh grilled octopus, and horiatiki (Greek village salad). For dessert, don't miss loukoumades (honey puffs) and galaktoboureko (custard pie). Pair everything with local wine — Greece has over 300 indigenous grape varieties!
